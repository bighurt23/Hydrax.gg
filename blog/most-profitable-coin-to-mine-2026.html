Why the "best coin" changes every single day

Two numbers move under your feet constantly:

  1. Coin price — your mined coins are only worth what the market pays today. A 20% price swing can flip the entire ranking overnight.
  2. Network difficulty — as more miners point hashrate at a coin, the network gets harder and your slice of the rewards shrinks. When a coin gets hot, profitability quietly erodes.

Put those together and the "most profitable coin" is a moving target. A coin that tops the list this morning can fall to fifth by tonight after a price dip and a difficulty bump. That's exactly why a static list goes stale the moment it's published — and why a live calculator beats any blog ranking, including this one.

The formula that ranks every coin

Under the hood, every "what to mine" tool is running the same math for each coin:

Daily Profit = (Your Hashrate ÷ Network Hashrate) × Daily Block Rewards × Coin Price − Power Cost

Break it down: your share of the network decides how many coins you earn, the coin's price converts those coins to dollars, and your electricity bill gets subtracted. Run that equation across every coin your hardware can mine, sort by the result, and the top row is your most profitable coin — for this exact moment. Do it again tomorrow and the order will likely be different.

Top GPU coins to watch in 2026

GPUs are the flexible choice — one rig can mine any of these and switch when profitability shifts. The usual contenders:

  • Ravencoin (RVN) — KawPow algorithm. The classic ASIC-resistant GPU coin with a deep, active network.
  • Ethereum Classic (ETC) — Etchash. High hashrate, large network, the natural home for ex-Ethereum GPU rigs.
  • Ergo (ERG) — Autolykos. Memory-friendly and power-efficient, often strong on watts-per-dollar.
  • Kaspa (KAS) — a fast, high-throughput chain that's also mined on ASICs, so GPU margins depend heavily on difficulty.
  • Monero (XMR) — RandomX, mined on your CPU rather than the GPU. It won't compete with a graphics card on raw output, but it's effectively free profit running alongside a GPU coin.

Top ASIC coins to watch in 2026

ASICs are purpose-built machines that crush one algorithm and do nothing else. They out-earn GPUs on their target coin but offer zero flexibility. The heavyweights:

  • Bitcoin (BTC) — SHA-256. The deepest, most competitive network on earth; profitability is razor-thin and lives and dies on your electricity rate.
  • Litecoin + Dogecoin (LTC/DOGE) — Scrypt, merged-mined. A single Scrypt ASIC earns both coins at once from the same work, which is a genuine edge.
  • Kaspa (KAS) — now firmly ASIC territory, with dedicated machines that have pushed GPU miners toward the margins.

The catch with ASICs: if your coin's price or difficulty turns against you, you can't pivot — the machine only knows one algorithm. That concentration risk is the core of the ASIC vs GPU decision, and it's worth reading before you spend four figures on a single-purpose box.

GPU or ASIC: which earns more?

It's not a clean win for either. A quick way to think about it:

GPU rigASIC
Earnings per machineLowerHigher (on its coin)
FlexibilityMine dozens of coinsOne algorithm only
Resale / reuseStrong (also a gaming card)Weak if the coin fades
Noise & heatManageableLoud, hot, industrial

If you want resilience and the ability to chase whatever's most profitable, GPUs win on adaptability. If you have very cheap power and want maximum raw output on one chain, an ASIC wins on brute force. For a deeper breakdown, see ASIC vs GPU Mining and our best mining GPUs of 2026.

Don't forget the costs that eat your ranking

  • Pool fees (~1%) and the occasional stale share trim the top line.
  • Hardware payback — a coin isn't truly profitable until the machine pays for itself; cheaper, used or Amazon Renewed GPUs reach breakeven far faster.
  • Difficulty creep — today's winner attracts miners, which raises difficulty and shrinks tomorrow's reward.
  • Downtime — no rig runs 100%; we default the calculator to 98% uptime so estimates aren't fantasy.

All of this is why calculating mining profit properly matters more than picking a coin off a list.
